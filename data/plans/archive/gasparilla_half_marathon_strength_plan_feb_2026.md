# Gasparilla Half Marathon Strength Training Plan
**February 22, 2026 | Coordinated with Running Plan**

---

## Plan Overview

**Duration:** 12 weeks (December 1, 2025 - February 22, 2026)
**Structure:** 2 sessions per week (Tuesday primary, Friday lighter)
**Focus:** Runner-specific strength supporting half marathon performance
**Critical Constraint:** NO WRIST PRESSURE (post-surgery recovery)

**Periodization:**
- **Recovery Phase (Weeks -2 to -1):** NO STRENGTH — Allow complete marathon recovery
- **Base Phase (Weeks 1-3):** Foundation strength, higher reps (12-15), lighter loads
- **Build Phase (Weeks 4-7):** Strength-endurance, moderate loads (8-12 reps)
- **Peak Phase (Weeks 8-9):** Maintenance + power, reduce volume 30%
- **Taper Phase (Week 10):** Minimal maintenance, mobility only

**Training Philosophy:**
- Full-body workouts every session (upper, lower, core)
- Runner-specific movements (single-leg stability, posterior chain, anti-rotation)
- Coordinate with running (lighter before quality runs, heavier after rest days)
- Progressive overload through phases
- Respect wrist constraint throughout

---

## Wrist Constraint Guidelines

### CANNOT DO:
- Push-ups (all variations)
- Planks (front-loaded positions)
- Front-loaded carries (farmer's walks, suitcase carries)
- Handstands or inverted positions
- Burpees
- Any exercise requiring weight-bearing on extended wrists

### CAN DO:
- Pull-ups, chin-ups, rows (all variations)
- Goblet holds (weight at chest, no wrist extension)
- Back-loaded carries (rucking, weighted vests)
- Deadlifts, RDLs, single-leg RDLs
- Squats, split squats, lunges
- Calf raises
- Core work on forearms (plank variations on forearms acceptable)
- Side planks on forearms
- Anti-rotation exercises (Pallof press, dead bugs, bird dogs)

**Modification Strategy:** Replace all wrist-loaded movements with pulling variations, goblet-hold positions, or back-loaded alternatives.

### Week 1 (Dec 15-21)
**Focus:** Return to strength training, assess movement quality

#### Tuesday, Dec 16 — Primary Lower Body + Core
**Warmup (10 min):**
- 5 min easy stationary bike or walking
- Hip circles × 10 each direction
- Leg swings (forward/back, side-to-side) × 10 each
- Bodyweight squats × 15
- Glute bridges × 15

**Main Session (35-40 min):**

**A1. Goblet Squat** — 3 × 12-15 reps
- Hold dumbbell or kettlebell at chest (goblet position)
- Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out
- Descend to parallel or below, chest up
- Rest: 60-90 sec
- *Purpose: Build quad/glute strength, foundational pattern*
- *Common mistakes: Knees caving in, heels lifting, forward lean*
- *Modification: Reduce depth if mobility limited*

**A2. Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift (RDL)** — 3 × 10-12 reps per leg
- Hold dumbbell in opposite hand (left leg = right hand holds weight)
- Hinge at hip, keep back flat, slight knee bend
- Lower weight toward floor while extending free leg back
- Rest: 60-90 sec
- *Purpose: Hamstring/glute strength, single-leg stability*
- *Common mistakes: Rounding back, rotating hips, losing balance*
- *Modification: Use lighter weight or no weight initially*

**B1. Bulgarian Split Squat** — 3 × 10-12 reps per leg
- Rear foot elevated on bench/chair
- Hold dumbbells at sides or goblet position
- Lower until front thigh parallel to ground
- Rest: 60 sec between legs
- *Purpose: Single-leg strength, quad/glute emphasis*
- *Common mistakes: Front knee caving in, torso leaning too far forward*
- *Modification: Reduce range of motion, bodyweight only*

**B2. Forearm Plank** — 3 × 30-45 sec holds
- Forearms on ground, elbows under shoulders
- Body in straight line from head to heels
- Engage core, squeeze glutes
- Rest: 45 sec
- *Purpose: Core anti-extension strength*
- *Common mistakes: Hips sagging, neck strain*
- *Modification: Knees on ground if needed*

**C1. Pallof Press** — 3 × 12-15 reps per side
- Stand sideways to resistance band anchor
- Hold band at chest, press forward, resist rotation
- Return to chest with control
- Rest: 30 sec between sides
- *Purpose: Core anti-rotation strength*
- *Common mistakes: Rotating torso, not fully extending*
- *Modification: Use lighter resistance*

**C2. Calf Raises (Single-Leg)** — 3 × 15-20 reps per leg
- Stand on edge of step, one foot
- Lower heel below step, then rise to toes
- Control both up and down phases
- Rest: 30 sec between legs
- *Purpose: Calf/Achilles strength, injury prevention*
- *Common mistakes: Bouncing, not full range*
- *Modification: Both legs if single-leg too difficult*

**Cooldown (5-10 min):**
- Light walking
- Hip flexor stretch × 30 sec each
- Hamstring stretch × 30 sec each
- Calf stretch × 30 sec each

#### Friday, Dec 19 — Light Maintenance

**A. Inverted Rows** — 3 × 10-12 reps

- Rest: 60 sec
- *Purpose: Upper back strength, pulling movement*
- *Common mistakes: Sagging hips, incomplete pull*

- Lift hips until body forms straight line
- Squeeze glutes at top, hold 2 sec
- Rest: 45 sec
- *Purpose: Glute activation, hip extension*
- *Common mistakes: Arching low back, not squeezing glutes*

**D. Side Forearm Plank** — 2 × 20-30 sec per side
- Forearm on ground, body in straight line
- Hips stacked, top arm on hip or extended
- Rest: 30 sec between sides
- *Purpose: Lateral core stability*
- *Common mistakes: Hips sagging, rolling forward*

## Exercise Library & Modifications

### Lower Body Exercises

#### Goblet Squat
**Setup:** Hold dumbbell or kettlebell at chest, elbows pointing down
**Execution:** Feet shoulder-width, descend until thighs parallel or below, drive through heels to stand
**Cues:** Chest up, knees track over toes, full depth
**Common Mistakes:** Knees caving in, heels lifting, excessive forward lean
**Modifications:**
- Box squat (touch box at bottom for depth control)
- Wider stance for mobility limitations
- Lighter weight for form focus

#### Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift (RDL)
**Setup:** Stand on one leg, hold weight in opposite hand
**Execution:** Hinge at hip with slight knee bend, lower weight toward floor while extending free leg back, maintain flat back
**Cues:** Hinge from hips not waist, keep back flat, squeeze glute to return
**Common Mistakes:** Rounding back, rotating hips open, bending knee excessively
**Modifications:**
- Touch wall or chair with free hand for balance
- No weight initially (bodyweight only)
- Reduce range of motion

#### Bulgarian Split Squat
**Setup:** Rear foot elevated on bench 12-18" high, front foot 2-3 feet forward
**Execution:** Lower until front thigh parallel to ground, drive through front heel to stand
**Cues:** Front knee tracks over toes, torso upright, push through heel
**Common Mistakes:** Front knee caving in, leaning too far forward, insufficient depth
**Modifications:**
- Lower bench height
- Bodyweight only
- Reverse lunge instead (no elevation)

#### Goblet Reverse Lunge
**Setup:** Hold dumbbell at chest, stand tall
**Execution:** Step back with one leg, lower until front thigh parallel, push through front heel to return
**Cues:** Front knee stable, torso upright, controlled descent
**Common Mistakes:** Forward knee caving in, stepping back too far/short
**Modifications:**
- Bodyweight only
- Walking lunge (forward instead of reverse)

#### Calf Raises (Single-Leg)
**Setup:** Stand on edge of step on one foot, other foot off ground
**Execution:** Lower heel below step level, rise to full toes, control both phases
**Cues:** Full range of motion, 2-sec pause at top, slow descent
**Common Mistakes:** Bouncing at bottom, incomplete range, not pausing at top
**Modifications:**
- Two-leg version
- Flat ground instead of elevated surface
- Holding wall for balance

#### Glute Bridge (Two-Leg)
**Setup:** Lie on back, feet flat, knees bent ~90 degrees, arms at sides
**Execution:** Lift hips until body forms straight line from knees to shoulders, squeeze glutes at top
**Cues:** Drive through heels, squeeze glutes hard, don't arch low back
**Common Mistakes:** Arching low back, not squeezing glutes, insufficient hip extension
**Modifications:**
- Single-leg version (more difficult)
- Add resistance band around knees
- Elevate feet on bench

---

### Upper Body Exercises

#### Pull-Up / Chin-Up
**Setup:** Hang from bar with arms fully extended
**Execution:** Pull until chin over bar, lower with control to full hang
**Cues:** Dead hang at bottom, full range, controlled descent
**Common Mistakes:** Kipping, incomplete range, only partial reps
**Modifications:**
- Assisted pull-up machine
- Resistance band assistance
- Negative pull-ups (jump up, lower slowly)
- Inverted rows instead

#### Inverted Rows
**Setup:** Bar at waist height (or suspension trainer), hang underneath with body straight
**Execution:** Pull chest to bar, lower with control
**Cues:** Body straight, squeeze shoulder blades, pull to chest
**Common Mistakes:** Sagging hips, incomplete pull, not squeezing shoulder blades
**Modifications:**
- Raise bar for easier
- Elevate feet for harder
- Single-arm version (advanced)

#### Dumbbell Bent-Over Row
**Setup:** Hinge at hips, back flat, knees slightly bent, dumbbells hanging
**Execution:** Pull dumbbells to ribs, elbows tight to body, squeeze shoulder blades
**Cues:** Flat back, pull to ribs not chest, elbows back not out
**Common Mistakes:** Rounding back, using momentum, pulling to chest instead of ribs
**Modifications:**
- Single-arm row (supported on bench)
- Lighter weight for form focus
- Seated cable row instead

---

### Core Exercises

#### Forearm Plank
**Setup:** Forearms on ground, elbows under shoulders, body straight
**Execution:** Hold position, engage core, squeeze glutes
**Cues:** Neutral spine, don't let hips sag, breathe normally
**Common Mistakes:** Hips sagging, butt in air, neck strain
**Modifications:**
- Knees on ground
- Shorter holds with more sets
- Elevated forearms (hands on bench)

#### Side Forearm Plank
**Setup:** Lie on side, forearm on ground, hips stacked
**Execution:** Lift hips until body forms straight line, hold
**Cues:** Hips stacked, don't rotate forward, engage obliques
**Common Mistakes:** Hips sagging, rolling forward, not stacking hips
**Modifications:**
- Bottom knee on ground
- Shorter holds
- Top leg forward (easier balance)

#### Side Forearm Plank with Hip Dip
**Setup:** Side forearm plank position
**Execution:** Lower hips toward floor without touching, lift back to plank
**Cues:** Controlled descent, obliques working, full range
**Common Mistakes:** Touching ground, losing hip alignment
**Modifications:**
- Reduce range of motion
- Static side plank instead

#### Pallof Press
**Setup:** Stand sideways to resistance band anchor, hold band at chest
**Execution:** Press band straight forward, resist rotation, return to chest with control
**Cues:** Don't rotate torso, press fully, control return
**Common Mistakes:** Rotating torso, incomplete extension, using too much resistance
**Modifications:**
- Lighter resistance band
- Half-kneeling position (more stable)
- Cable machine instead of band

#### Dead Bug
**Setup:** Lie on back, arms extended toward ceiling, knees bent 90 degrees
**Execution:** Extend opposite arm and leg (right arm + left leg), keep low back pressed to floor
**Cues:** Low back stays down, slow movement, breathe normally
**Common Mistakes:** Arching low back, moving too fast, holding breath
**Modifications:**
- Arms only (don't extend legs)
- Legs only (keep arms stable)
- Smaller range of motion

#### Bird Dog
**Setup:** Start on hands and knees, hands under shoulders, knees under hips
**Execution:** Extend opposite arm and leg (right arm + left leg), hold, return, switch
**Cues:** Don't rotate hips, keep back flat, hold extension
**Common Mistakes:** Rotating hips, arching back, not fully extending
**Modifications:**
- Arm only or leg only
- Shorter holds
- Reduce range of motion

## Progressive Overload Strategy

### Base Phase (Weeks 1-3):
**Method:** Higher reps (12-15), lighter loads (60-70% max)
**Progression:**
- Week 1: Establish baseline loads and form
- Week 2: Increase weight 5-10% if Week 1 felt easy
- Week 3: Increase reps to top of range (15 reps)

### Build Phase (Weeks 4-7):
**Method:** Moderate loads (75-80% max), moderate reps (8-12)
**Progression:**
- Week 4: Increase weight 10-15% from Week 3, reduce reps to 8-10
- Week 5: Increase reps by 1-2 per set OR increase weight 2.5-5 lbs
- Week 6: Increase weight 5 lbs, reduce reps to bottom of range
- Week 7: Maintain Week 6 loads, focus on quality

### Peak Phase (Weeks 8-9):
**Method:** Reduced volume (30% fewer sets), maintain loads, add explosive elements
**Progression:**
- Week 8: Reduce sets from 4 to 3, maintain weight, add explosive variations
- Week 9: Bodyweight or very light loads, reduce to 2 sets, minimal volume

### Taper Phase (Week 10):
**Method:** NO STRENGTH WORK, mobility only
**Progression:** Complete rest from resistance training

## Coordination with Running Schedule

### Key Principles:
1. **Heavy strength after rest days or easy runs** (Tuesday follows Monday easy run or rest)
2. **Light strength before weekend long runs** (Friday light session, Saturday long run)
3. **No heavy lower body work within 48 hours of quality runs** (Tuesday strength → Thursday quality run = 48+ hours)
4. **Reduce strength volume during running peak weeks** (Week 8-9)
5. **Eliminate strength during race week** (Week 10)

### Weekly Coordination:
| Day | Running | Strength |
|-----|---------|----------|
| **Mon** | Easy run or REST | — |
| **Tue** | REST | Primary strength (heavier lower body safe) |
| **Wed** | Easy run | — |
| **Thu** | Quality workout (tempo/intervals) | — |
| **Fri** | Light strength (avoid DOMS before long run) | Light maintenance |
| **Sat** | Long run | — |
| **Sun** | Mobility/yoga | — |

**Rationale:**
- Tuesday strength is 48+ hours before Thursday quality run (adequate recovery)
- Friday light strength is 24 hours before Saturday long run (minimal interference)
- Monday easy run or rest sets up Tuesday for heavier loads
- No strength competes with running quality sessions

## Recovery & Adaptation Guidelines

### Signs to Reduce Strength Load:
- **Excessive DOMS** interfering with running form (>2 days soreness)
- **Elevated resting HR** >5 bpm above baseline
- **Poor sleep** (<6.5 hours for 2+ nights)
- **Persistent fatigue** throughout the day
- **Struggling with running paces** that should feel manageable

### Modification Options:
**If compromised:**
1. Skip Tuesday primary session, do Friday light session only
2. Reduce weight by 10-20%, maintain reps and form
3. Convert Tuesday session to light maintenance (same as Friday)
4. Take complete rest from strength for 1 week if severely fatigued

**If running quality session moved:**
- Ensure 48+ hours between heavy lower body strength and quality run
- Example: If Thursday quality → Friday, move Tuesday strength to Wednesday light session only

- **Friday:** REST or mobility only (no resistance work)
- **Volume Reduction:** ~60-70% reduction from peak

### Week 10 (Race Week):
- **Tuesday:** REST or 20 min light mobility (no weights)
- **Friday:** REST
- **No Strength Work:** Complete elimination of resistance training

**Rationale:**
- Strength adaptations from previous 9 weeks are maintained for 2-3 weeks without training
- Muscle damage from strength work interferes with race-day freshness
- A-race protocol demands maximum rest and recovery
- Mobility work maintains range of motion without creating fatigue
