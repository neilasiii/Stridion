## Exercise Library

### PTT-Specific Exercises (Daily Focus)

**1. Towel Scrunches**
- Seated, bare foot on towel
- Scrunch towel toward you using toes
- 3 sets × 15 reps per foot
- **Cue:** Engage arch, feel medial ankle activation

**2. Single-Leg Heel Raises (Eccentric Focus)**
- Stand on step, ball of foot on edge
- Rise on both feet, lower slowly on one (3-5 sec descent)
- 3 sets × 8-12 reps per leg
- **Cue:** Control descent, feel posterior tibialis working along medial ankle

**3. Toe Yoga (Foot Intrinsics)**
- Seated or standing
- Lift big toe while keeping other toes down, then reverse
- 2 sets × 10 reps per foot
- **Cue:** Isolate toe movements, build arch control

**4. Banded Ankle Inversions**
- Loop resistance band around forefoot
- Anchor band to stable object (lateral side)
- Slowly invert foot (turn sole inward) against resistance
- 3 sets × 15 reps per foot
- **Cue:** Controlled movement, feel medial ankle engagement

**5. Standing Arch Doming**
- Barefoot, standing on one leg
- Create arch by "pulling" heel toward toes without curling toes
- Hold 10 sec × 6 reps per foot
- **Cue:** Shorten foot, activate intrinsics

### Static Stretching Sequences

**Post-Run Static Sequence (10 min)**
1. **Standing Calf Stretch** (soleus emphasis): 60 sec per side
   - Back knee bent, heel down, lean into wall
2. **Standing Calf Stretch** (gastrocnemius): 60 sec per side
   - Back knee straight, heel down, lean into wall
3. **Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch**: 60 sec per side
   - Squeeze glute, tuck pelvis under
4. **Pigeon Pose** (modified): 90 sec per side
   - Front shin parallel to mat, back leg extended
5. **Seated Hamstring Stretch**: 60 sec per side
   - Flex foot, hinge from hips
6. **Figure-4 Stretch** (supine): 60 sec per side
   - Pull knee toward chest, feel glute stretch
7. **Supine Spinal Twist**: 60 sec per side
   - Knee across body, shoulders on ground

**Cues for All Static Stretches:**
- Hold steady, no bouncing
- Breathe deeply (4 sec inhale, 6 sec exhale)
- Stretch to mild tension (5-6/10), not pain
- Relax into stretch over time

### PNF Stretching (Sunday Sessions)

**Contract-Relax Method:**
1. Move into stretch position (passive)
2. Contract muscle against resistance (isometric) for 5-6 sec
3. Relax and breathe
4. Deepen stretch for 20-30 sec
5. Repeat 2-3 cycles

**Key Targets:**
- **Hamstrings:** Supine, leg elevated, partner or strap resistance
- **Hip Flexors:** Kneeling lunge, press shin into ground during contraction
- **Calves:** Seated or standing, press forefoot into wall during contraction

### Breathing & Recovery Protocols

**Diaphragmatic Breathing (Daily, 5 min)**
- Supine or seated
- One hand on chest, one on belly
- Inhale through nose (4 sec), belly rises (chest still)
- Exhale through mouth (6 sec), belly falls
- 10 cycles
- **Purpose:** Downregulate nervous system, improve recovery

**Box Breathing (High-Stress Days)**
- Inhale 4 sec
- Hold 4 sec
- Exhale 4 sec
- Hold 4 sec
- Repeat 5-10 cycles
- **Use:** Before bed, post-quality workouts, when feeling overwhelmed

---

### Hip Mobility Drills

**Essential Hip Sequence (15 min)**
1. **90/90 Hip Stretch**: 90 sec per side
   - Front leg 90° internally rotated, back leg 90° externally rotated
   - Sit tall, lean forward over front shin
2. **Cossack Squats**: 10 per side
   - Wide stance, shift weight side to side, keep feet flat
3. **Fire Hydrants**: 15 per side
   - Hands and knees, lift bent leg out to side
4. **Hip CARs (Controlled Articular Rotations)**: 5 per side
   - Standing, lift knee up, rotate leg in large circle (hip socket), reverse
5. **Banded Hip Abductions**: 20 per side
   - Loop band around ankles, step laterally with control
6. **Banded Clamshells**: 20 per side
   - Side-lying, knees bent, open top knee against band

---

### Thoracic Spine Mobility

**Thoracic Mobility Sequence (10 min)**
1. **Cat-Cow**: 10 slow cycles
   - Hands and knees, arch and round spine
2. **Thread the Needle**: 8 per side
   - Hands and knees, thread arm under body, rotate thoracic spine
3. **Quadruped Thoracic Rotations**: 10 per side
   - Hands and knees, one hand behind head, rotate elbow toward ceiling
4. **Foam Roller Thoracic Extensions**: 10 reps
   - Foam roller across mid-back, hands support head, extend over roller
5. **Side-Lying Thoracic Windmills**: 8 per side
   - Knees bent, top arm sweeps overhead and behind

**Purpose:** Improve breathing mechanics, reduce compensatory lower back stress during running

## PTT Flare-Up Protocol

If you experience PTT symptoms (medial ankle pain, arch discomfort, pain with push-off):

**Immediate Actions:**
1. **STOP the aggravating activity** - do not run through PTT pain
2. Ice medial ankle: 15 min on, 45 min off (repeat 3x daily)
3. Compression sock or ankle sleeve
4. Reduce or eliminate running for 24-48 hours

**Modified Mobility Protocol (Daily until resolved):**

*Morning (10 min)*
- Plantar fascia release (ball): 3 min per foot
- Gentle calf stretching (soleus focus): 2 min per side
- Towel scrunches: 2 × 12 per foot
- Toe yoga: 2 × 8 per foot

*Evening (15 min)*
- Ice: 15 min
- Gentle foam rolling: Calves only, very light pressure (5 min)
- Calf stretching (both gastroc and soleus): 3 min per side
- Standing arch doming: 5 × 10 sec per foot
- Banded ankle inversions (light resistance): 2 × 10 per foot

**Return to Running:**
- Wait until no pain with walking, stairs, single-leg stance
- Resume with 50% volume, easy pace only
- Gradual progression over 5-7 days
- Continue PTT exercises daily for 2 weeks post-symptoms

**Communication:**
- Notify running coach immediately if symptoms persist >48 hours
- May need to adjust training plan
- Do NOT push through - PTT can become chronic if ignored

## Modification Guidelines

**If Sleep <6 Hours:**
- Reduce micro-session time by 50%
- Focus on breathing and gentle foam rolling only
- Skip any aggressive stretching or PNF work
- Prioritize recovery over stimulus

**If RHR Elevated >5 bpm:**
- Sunday session: Light foam rolling + breathing only (20 min)
- Daily sessions: Optional, only if feeling good
- Increase focus on nervous system downregulation (breathing work)

**If Acute Soreness/Fatigue:**
- Replace scheduled session with gentle foam rolling + breathing
- No PNF stretching (requires muscle contraction)
- Extended static holds (90-120 sec) for tension release
- Add extra rest day if needed

**If Illness/Injury:**
- Consult running coach before resuming mobility work
- May maintain breathing protocols even when unable to exercise
- Gentle movement may aid recovery (case-dependent)

## Progress Tracking

**Weekly Assessment (Sundays during primary session):**

1. **Subjective Feel:**
   - Muscle tension: Low / Moderate / High
   - Joint stiffness: None / Mild / Moderate / Severe
   - PTT symptoms: None / Mild / Moderate / Severe
   - Energy level: Low / Moderate / High

2. **Functional Tests:**
   - Single-leg balance (eyes closed): Target 30 sec per leg
   - Bodyweight squat depth: Assess hip/ankle mobility
   - Thoracic rotation: Assess upper back mobility

3. **Foam Rolling Tenderness:**
   - Note particularly tender areas (may indicate fatigue or overload)
   - Track if tenderness decreases week-to-week (sign of recovery)

**Monthly Review (1st Sunday of each month):**
- Review PTT symptom frequency
- Assess mobility improvements (squat depth, hip rotation, etc.)
- Adjust sessions if needed based on training load changes

## Key Reminders

1. **DO NOT SKIP post-run routines** - 5-10 min investment prevents hours of injury recovery
2. **Sunday sessions are NON-NEGOTIABLE** - primary maintenance for the week
3. **PTT exercises are DAILY** - consistency prevents flare-ups
4. **Listen to your body** - if something hurts, modify or skip
5. **Breathing work is training** - nervous system recovery is real recovery
6. **Quality over quantity** - 10 focused minutes beats 30 distracted minutes
7. **Foam rolling is not torture** - if it's unbearable, you're pressing too hard
8. **Static stretching post-run, dynamic pre-run** - never reverse this
9. **Taper week is LIGHT maintenance** - do not add new stretches or aggressive work
10. **Race week priority: Feel fresh and loose** - not worked over and sore
